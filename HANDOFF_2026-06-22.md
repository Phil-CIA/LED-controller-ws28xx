## Phase Completed: Reverse-Polarity Protection Debugging

### Problem Statement
Q1 (BSP322PH6327 P-channel MOSFET SOT-223) in the reverse-polarity protection stage was not conducting properly:
- Measured **VIN_REVPROT = 4.62V** at 5V input (should be ~5V for full conduction)
- Indicated insufficient gate-source bias voltage to enhance the PMOS channel
- Board was relying on expensive body-diode conduction rather than efficient MOSFET channel

### Root Cause Identified
Gate bias network (R5/R6 divider to ground) was producing insufficient negative gate voltage:
- **Original:** R5 = 10kΩ (to VIN_RAW), R6 = 100kΩ (to GND)  
- This gave $V_G = \frac{5V \times 10k}{10k + 100k} = 0.45V$
- Therefore $V_{GS} = 0.45V - 5V = -0.45V$ (too shallow for PMOS enhancement)

### Solution Implemented: R5/R6 Swap
**Resistor values swapped to increase pull-down strength:**

| Component | Before | After | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|-------|---------|
| **R5** | 10kΩ (PCM_JLCPCB C17414) | **100kΩ** (PCM_JLCPCB C149504) | Weaker pull to VIN_RAW |
| **R6** | 100kΩ (PCM_JLCPCB C149504) | **10kΩ** (PCM_JLCPCB C17414) | Stronger pull to GND |

**New divider calculation at 5V input:**
$$V_G = \frac{5V \times 10k}{100k + 10k} = 0.45V$$
$$V_{GS} = 0.45V - 5V = -4.55V$$

This provides **~4.55V of gate-source enhancement voltage**, strongly pulling current through the MOSFET channel instead of relying on body diode.

## Next Phase: Board Connection & Measurement Validation

### Objective
Verify that the resistor swap produces the expected gate-source voltage and increases VIN_REVPROT from 4.62V toward 5V.

### Hardware Setup Required
1. **Connect power to VIN_RAW (5V nominal)**
2. **Measure at Q1 gate (net: Q1_GATE)**
   - Expected: ~0.45V (divider output)
3. **Measure VIN_REVPROT (after Q1 drain)**
   - Current state: 4.62V ❌
   - Expected after fix: ~4.9–5.0V ✅
4. **Optional: Measure Vgs directly across Q1**
   - Expected: ~-4.55V (gate at 0.45V, source at 5V)

### Interpretation of Results

#### Success Scenario
If **VIN_REVPROT measures ≥4.9V** at 5V input:
- Gate bias fix worked correctly
- PMOS is now conducting in enhancement mode with good channel resistance
- Proceed to full board testing (load testing, reverse-polarity test)

#### Partial Success (VIN_REVPROT = 4.62–4.8V)
- Indicates gate bias improved but PMOS still not fully enhanced
- Check for:
  - PCB layout issues (high trace resistance on Q1 gate pull-down)
  - Component soldering quality or part substitution
  - Gate-to-source leakage path

#### Failure (VIN_REVPROT still ≈4.62V or lower)
- Gate bias swap alone did not resolve the issue
- **Likely secondary cause:** D/S pins swapped on Q1 package or symbol
  - BSP322PH6327 footprint may have drain/source reversed
  - Requires symbol/footprint rework and PCB trace remapping
  - Contact board design reference to verify pin assignments
